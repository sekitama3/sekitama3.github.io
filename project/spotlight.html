Temple Spotlight - London England


Dedicated on September 7, 1958 by President David O. McKay, the London England temple has been serving Latter-day Saints in both England and Wales for over 60 years. Though the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints was established in Preston in 1837, this temple was the fourteenth temple to be dedicated, over 100 years later. The main cause for this wide gap is due to early converts immigrating to the United States, and ultimately Salt Lake City, Utah, until the 1950s, when church leaders admonished saints to build up the church in their native lands. The London England temple was the first temple in the United Kingdom and has had some changes made to it since its original dedication. In 1992, President Gordon B. Hinckley rededicated this temple and sixteen years later, in 2008, the angel Moroni was placed on the spire. You can find out more information about this temple here: churchofjesuschrist.org
